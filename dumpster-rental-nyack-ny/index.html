Frequently Asked Questions

Our business is very image-conscious. Are your dumpsters clean and presentable?

We understand that brand image is paramount. We pride ourselves on having one of the best-maintained fleets in the industry. Our roll off containers are regularly serviced and painted to ensure they present a clean, professional appearance on your commercial property.

We need to do our renovation after hours. Can you accommodate nighttime or weekend service?

Yes. We specialize in working with commercial clients whose operations cannot be disrupted. We will work with you to schedule discreet, after-hours or weekend delivery and pickup times that align with your project’s schedule.

What do we do about old computers, printers, and other e-waste from our office cleanout?

While you can toss some electronics into the dumpster, we strongly encourage a more environmentally conscious approach. We recommend partnering with a dedicated e-waste recycling company for large quantities of computers and monitors. The dumpster is best used for furniture, fixtures, and general debris.

Our restaurant is getting rid of old ovens and prep tables. Can they go in the dumpster?

Absolutely. Heavy stainless steel equipment, ovens, and tables can be loaded into our dumpsters. The only exception is for equipment with refrigerants, like walk-in freezers or refrigerators, which must be professionally drained of Freon before disposal.

How do we ensure the dumpster rental doesn't disrupt other businesses in our plaza or office park?

Communication is key. We will work with you and your property manager to identify the least disruptive placement for the roll off rental, typically in a designated loading zone or service alley. Our professional drivers are trained to operate quickly and quietly to minimize any impact on your neighbors.

Nyack, NY Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Nyack (/ˈnaɪ.æk/ i) is a village located primarily in the town of Orangetown in Rockland County, New York, United States. Incorporated in 1872, it retains a very small western section in Clarkstown. The village had a population of 7,265 as of the 2020 census. It is a suburb of New York City lying approximately 15 miles (24 km) north of the Manhattan boundary near the west bank of the Hudson River, situated north of South Nyack, east of Central Nyack, south of Upper Nyack, and southeast of Valley Cottage.
